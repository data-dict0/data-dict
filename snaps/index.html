The end of parliamentary courtesy?


27 Aug. 2024

This week's graphic, we look closer at the so-called "parliamentary courtesy" extended by Congress to presidents and vice-presidents during budget deliberations.

Congress extends this courtesy by forgoing lengthy deliberations on the budget proposals of the Office of the President and Vice President. Media typically report these deliberations to last merely minutes. This year however, legislators appeared to have ditched this courtesy on the case of Vice President Sara Duterte.

On Aug. 27, the House appropriations committee questioned Duterte during her office's budget deliberations, which lasted for over five hours.
